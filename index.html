About Me

Hello everyone. First I would like to introduce myself. I am Fatimah Ibrahim. I was born in SA in Makkah city. I graduated from the college of Qur’anic Studies. After that I worked as a teacher in a high school for 4 years. When I taught, I followed my old teachers’ methods, which can be described as traditional, boring way to deliver information to students.

And honestly speaking, I felt bored during the classes more than my students. Even when I was using the PowerPoint or worksheets, I wasn’t trained enough to use them effectively. When I started studying in the United State to earn my master’s degree, I saw the differences of the teaching methods from the first class in the ELI. I got positively shocked how enjoyable the classes were.

Challenges

My journey in the US has had lots of challenges. I’ve been a mother for a boy before I came here. Studying a second new language was the biggest pressure on me in the beginning. Then I had my second child after a year of being a grad student. It was a great and terrible thing at the same time. The cultural differences, the American lifestyle, the weather, and the food, were all difficult parts of my experience. Regarding the academic side, when my leg got broken last winter, I stayed home for three months, I couldn’t attend my classes that impacted my ability to understand correctly all the materials and assignments of that quarter.
